About SongSAT

"From Bands to a Band"

SongSAT is a tool to share the beauty of the world in different mediums, expressing the wonders of satellite imagery through audio. This allows the beauty of satellite imagery to communicate to an audience with visual impairments to enjoy the wonders of the world from above too, or to be used by musicians to aid with musical writing blocks.

Our team produced an algorithm, SongSAT, to convert four distinct geographical areas (Grasslands, forest, coastal/water areas, and mountainous areas) into songs with distinct, recognizable musical patterns that play back using MIDI. SongSAT uses the geographical location of the image and compares coordinates within the image to the GLCF dataset, a global land use dataset generated from NASA's MODIS satellite data. It is also compared to a second layer, a global mountain range dataset provided by the United Nations. The most commonly occuring landcover type from the sampling is selected, and then the image data, along with the predominant land cover class, is then passed into the SongSAT algorithm. SongSAT turns the data into music by quantizing the 0-255 data range in LandSAT pixels to a smaller range, which then becomes the degrees of a scale. For each landscape, a different, appropriate scale is applied. From there, each bar is generated using a selection of pre-defined rhythms, and SongSAT simply creates the order of notes. In order to properly capture the feeling in each landscape, we took the MIDI files outputted and brought them into MuseScore, where we used different instruments and virtual instrument plugins to further enhance the auditory experience.

We're proud to announce that this algorithm is 100% open source, and you can view the code at: http://github.com/mcvittal/SongSAT
